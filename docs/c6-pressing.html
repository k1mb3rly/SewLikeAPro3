Parts 7 & 8: Pressing & Quick Reference

The directional pressing technique that replaces topstitching, setting the lapel crease, and the full checklist for today's work.

Part 7: Pressing — The Key to a Professional Finish

This is the technique that replaces topstitching. Done correctly, your collar and lapel will have clean, crisp edges with no visible seam from the outside — without a single topstitch.

Step 1: Press the Front Seam Open

  1. Use a pressing ham to give you a raised edge.
  2. Before turning the facing to the inside, press the entire front seam open (both allowances spread apart), just like any other seam.
  3. You won't be able to reach all the way into the corners — that's fine. Get as far as you can.
  4. This pre-pressing flattens the seam so that when you flip and press it for real, the edge is already set and you don't have to fight with it.

Step 2: Turn and Press with Direction

Turn the facing to the inside. Now do the final press. This is where direction matters.

The Critical Pressing Rule

FROM THE BREAK DOWN (below the lapel fold, toward the hem):
→ Press from the outside of the jacket.
→ Tuck the seam allowance behind/underneath so it's invisible from the front.
→ You should NOT see the seam from the jacket exterior.

FROM THE BREAK UP (above the lapel fold, around the collar):
→ Press from the inside of the jacket (the side that will show when the lapel folds back).
→ Tuck the seam allowance under so it's hidden from the lapel's visible face.

WHY: Below the break, the outside of the jacket shows. Above the break, the inside shows (because the lapel folds back). You always press from whichever side will be visible, tucking the seam behind.

Step 3: Press the Lapel Crease

  1. Once the entire front edge and collar are pressed with the seam hidden, lay the jacket flat.
  2. Find your break clips.
  3. Fold the lapel back at the break, the way it will sit when worn.
  4. Press in the actual fold crease at the break.

No Topstitching Needed

This pressing technique is what high-end blazers use instead of topstitching. The pre-pressing (Step 1) sets the edge, the directional pressing (Step 2) hides the seam, and the break crease (Step 3) sets the lapel fold. Combined with the interfacing, this gives you a clean, professional finish that holds its shape.

Part 8: Today's Checklist

  • Set the break position on your paper pattern (move button/buttonhole if needed)
  • Notch the break on both jacket fronts AND both front facings
  • Sew under collar center back seam. Press open.
  • Sew back neck facing center back seam. Press open.
  • Sew facing family shoulders. Press open.
  • Attach under collar to jacket neckline (pivot at shoulders). Press open.
  • Attach top collar to facing neckline (pivot at shoulders). Press open.
  • Grade collar seam allowances (trim one side to half width)
  • Pin families right sides together: collar points, collar curve, notch corners, break notches, fronts
  • Sew the perimeter (start ½″ in from hem edge each side, pivot at all corners)
  • Clip: 6 diagonal corners, collar curve, 90° notch corners, deepen break clips
  • Clean out all corners with point turner
  • Press front seam open (on ham)
  • Turn, then directional press: outside below break, inside above break
  • Press the lapel fold crease at the break
