Part 1: Body Assembly — Shell & Lining

The big picture, prerequisites, and step-by-step body seam assembly for both the shell jacket and lining.

The Big Picture

You are building two complete jackets — the shell (main fabric) and the lining — which are assembled separately and joined together later. Think of them as two parallel construction tracks.

Prerequisites Before Starting Body Assembly

  • All darts sewn and pressed
  • All welt pockets sewn
  • All interfacing fused

Construction Sequence This Class

  1. Lay out and pin all body seams (shell and lining separately)
  2. Sew all body seams (½″ seam allowance, right sides together)
  3. Press ALL seams open
  4. Add basting stitches to sleeve caps
  5. Ease/ruche the sleeve caps
  6. Pin and sew sleeves into armholes
  7. Sew sleeve head into armhole (machine)
  8. Hand-sew shoulder pad into armhole

Lining Layout & Assembly

  • Leave a 4″ turning hole in the center back lining seam: start 5″ up from the hem, leave a 4″ gap, then continue sewing. You will use this hole later to turn the jacket right side out.
  • There is NO hole in the shell jacket center back seam.
  • The front lining dart is absorbed into the facing seam — it is not a separate dart to sew.
  • Mirror left/right sleeve pairs carefully when laying out — lining fabric looks the same on both sides, so it's easy to cut two of the same hand.

Turning Hole Dimensions

Measure 5″ up from the hem, mark your gap start. Leave 4″ unsewn, then resume. This gives you enough room to reach in and pull the jacket through when turning.

Shell Jacket Body Assembly

Pinning

  • Pin center back, side seams, and shoulders — right sides together.
  • Pinning order: one end, other end, middle, then middle of each gap. This distributes ease evenly.

Sewing

  • Sew all seams at ½″.
  • Press ALL seams open after sewing.

Shell Sleeves

  • Confirm interfacing is on the wrong side of each sleeve piece.
  • Mirror the elbow curve correctly: tall to tall, short to short when joining upper and lower sleeve.
  • Sew at ½″, press open.
