Parts 3 & 4: Attaching the Lining

Sewing the lining to the facing edges around the upper perimeter, then sewing the lining to the jacket at the bottom hem — including how to handle the hem extension lip.

Part 3: Attaching the Lining — Upper Edge

You're sewing the lining to the jacket around the facing edges and back neck first, then the bottom hem separately.

Setup

  1. Lay the jacket right side out, back facing you.
  2. Flip the facing edges outward so you can see the facing's raw inner edge all the way around (front facing, back neck facing, other front facing).
  3. Take the lining and place it right sides together on the facing, with the right side of the lining facing down onto the jacket.
  4. Move all sleeves completely out of the way.

Pinning Order

  1. Bottom corners: match the bottom of each front facing to the bottom of the corresponding lining edge. Pin.
  2. Shoulders: match each lining shoulder seam to the facing shoulder seam. Pin. Keep seam allowances pressed open.
  3. Center back: match lining center back to facing center back. Pin.
  4. Fill in between all major points: middle, middle, middle.
  5. If you removed the front lining dart into the facing seam, that hourglass curve will be along this edge. It will pin fine — just match up the slightly different curves.

Sewing the Upper Edge

  1. Start at the bottom edge of one facing. Backstitch.
  2. Sew at ½″ seam allowance all the way around: up the facing, across the back neck, down the other facing.
  3. Keep the bulk of the jacket out from under the presser foot — this is the trickiest part, just managing the volume of fabric.
  4. Make sure seam allowances are open wherever you cross them.
  5. Backstitch at the other bottom corner.

Part 4: Attaching the Lining — Bottom Hem

Now you're sewing the bottom edge of the lining to the bottom edge of the jacket.

The Hem Extension

Remember the elongated lip at the bottom of the front jacket pattern? That extra length is what creates the overhang where the jacket folds over to meet the lining.

Push Down, Don't Stretch Out

Do NOT stretch the jacket's long lip out to match the lining edge. Instead, push the extra fabric DOWN so the raw edge of the lip is flush with the raw edge of the lining. This will create a small bubble/fold on the jacket side. That is correct — don't try to flatten it out. It gets tucked in after turning.

Pinning & Sewing the Bottom Hem

  1. Match center back of the jacket hem to center back of the lining hem. Pin.
  2. Match side seams to side seams. Pin. Keep seam allowances open.
  3. At each front corner: push the hem extension lip down so its raw edge aligns with the lining raw edge. Pin. Accept the bubble.
  4. Fill in between all pins (middle, middle, middle). Make sure welt pockets are out of the way.
  5. Sew straight across the entire bottom at ½″ seam allowance. Keep the jacket bubbles pushed out of the way of the presser foot.
