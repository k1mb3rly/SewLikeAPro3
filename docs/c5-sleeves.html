Part 2: Setting In Sleeves

Five steps to set in a professional sleeve — from identifying left vs. right, through basting and easing the cap, to pinning and sewing the final seam.

Step A — Identify Left vs. Right

What You SeeWhat It Means
Two notches on the seamBack of the sleeve
One notch on the seamFront of the sleeve
Longer seam (under sleeve)Back of the sleeve

Use these cues together to confirm orientation before pinning.

Step B — Basting Stitches on the Sleeve Cap

  • Set stitch length to maximum (5 on most machines).
  • Sew ¼″ from the edge of the sleeve cap.
  • Stitch from notch to notch — only across the curved cap top, not the full circumference.
  • Do NOT backstitch — leave long thread tails on both ends. You'll use these tails to pull and ease the cap.

Two Rows is Better

Some sewists run two rows of basting (⅛″ and ¼″ from the edge) for more control when easing. Either row can be pulled; use whichever gives you the smoothest result.

Step C — Ease the Cap

  1. Hold the sleeve cap with both notches visible.
  2. Pull one long basting tail to begin gathering.
  3. Push fullness toward the center from both sides simultaneously.
  4. The goal is a "shelf" — the cap should curl slightly inward at the top when viewed from the side. This is the shape that creates a smooth, rounded shoulder when set in.
  5. Distribute the ease evenly — no tucks or abrupt folds.

What You're Looking For

Hold the eased cap up and look at the edge from the side. It should curve gently inward like the rounded top of a dome — not lie flat and not fold over. That curve is what gives the sleeve its shape.

Step D — Pin Sleeve into Armhole

Work right sides together. Pin in this order:

  1. Undersleeve center notch to the side seam of the jacket body.
  2. Center cap notch (top of cap) to the shoulder seam.
  3. Lower underarm sections — these are flat with no ease, pin them smoothly.
  4. Distribute the eased fullness evenly across the top of the cap between the notches. There should be no tucks, puckers, or folds — only smooth fullness lying toward the sleeve side.

Pin Generously

Use more pins than you think you need across the eased section. The more you pin, the less the fullness shifts when you move to the machine.

Step E — Sew the Sleeve In

  • Switch stitch length back to normal first — sewing with basting-length stitches by mistake is a common error.
  • Start at the underarm (the flat section with no ease) and backstitch to secure.
  • Sew at ½″ all the way around the armhole.
  • Keep the eased fullness to the right of the presser foot so you can see it and avoid stitching tucks into it.
  • At body seams (side seam, shoulder seam), the seam allowances must lie flat and open as you sew past them.
  • Do NOT press the seam open — press the seam allowance toward the sleeve. This supports the sleeve head and shoulder pad that go in next.
