Part 3: Sleeve Head & Shoulder Pad

How to install the sleeve head by machine and hand-sew the shoulder pad so the jacket holds its professional shape.

Installing the Sleeve Head

The sleeve head is a folded strip of soft material that supports the sleeve cap from the inside, preventing it from collapsing inward at the shoulder.

Step-by-Step

  1. Fold the sleeve head in half and mark its center point.
  2. Align the center mark to the shoulder seam of the armhole.
  3. Orientation: the fabric/muslin side faces the sleeve; the batting side faces the seam allowance.
  4. Sew at ¼″ by machine along the full length of the sleeve head, backstitching at both start and end.

Thin Sleeve Heads

If your sleeve heads feel very thin, layer two together before installing. You can also add a strip of muslin on the batting side for extra support.

Installing the Shoulder Pad

Shoulder pads give the jacket its structure at the shoulder. They are hand-sewn — not machine-sewn — and attach only to the seam allowance, not to the jacket body itself.

Step-by-Step

  1. Place the pad flat side down inside the jacket, centered on the shoulder seam.
  2. Align the center of the pad to the shoulder seam.
  3. Hand-sew with a running stitch along the length of the pad, attaching it to the seam allowance only.
  4. The sides of the pad remain free and unsewn — the pad should float slightly, not be stitched flat to the jacket body.

Why Seam Allowance Only?

Stitching the pad directly to the jacket body would create visible stitch marks on the outside. Attaching only to the seam allowance keeps the exterior clean.
