Part 4: Garment Care & Remaining Schedule

Care instructions for the finished blazer, what's coming in the next classes, and your checklist for this week.

Garment Care

Dry Clean Only

Spot clean by hand if necessary. Do NOT machine wash, hand wash, or submerge in water.

Water damages the construction of a tailored jacket in multiple ways:

  • Fusible interfacing — water can cause delamination (the glue releasing from the fabric)
  • Welt pockets — the structure can distort or collapse
  • Sleeve heads — the batting shifts and loses its shape
  • Shoulder pads — the muslin and batting can shrink unevenly

This Week's Checklist

  • Lay out and pin all lining body seams (leave 4″ turning hole in center back, starting 5″ up from hem)
  • Sew lining body seams at ½″, press all seams open
  • Assemble lining sleeves, press seams open
  • Lay out and pin all shell body seams (no turning hole)
  • Sew shell body seams at ½″, press all seams open
  • Assemble shell sleeves, press seams open
  • Basting stitches on all 4 sleeve caps (¼″ from edge, max stitch length, no backstitch)
  • Ease/ruche all 4 caps until they form a smooth dome (the "shelf")
  • Pin and sew all 4 sleeves into armholes (½″, normal stitch length; press toward sleeve)
  • Sew sleeve heads into shell armholes (machine, ¼″)
  • Hand-sew shoulder pads into shell armholes (running stitch to seam allowance only)
