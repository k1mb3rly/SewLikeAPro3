Parts 7 & 8: Hand Tacks & Quick Reference

Securing the lining in place with hand tacks at the armhole and bottom hem, plus the full checklist for this week.

Part 7: Hand Tacks to Secure the Lining

Without tacks, the lining can shift, pull away, or come out when you put your arm through the sleeve. Three locations get tacked.

Seam Allowances Only

All tacks go through seam allowances only — never through the visible garment fabric on either side.

Tack 1: Underarm / Armhole

This prevents the lining sleeve from pulling out when you take the jacket off.

  1. Thread a needle with doubled thread. Knot the end.
  2. Reach through the turning hole. Find the underarm area where the side seam meets the armhole.
  3. Grab the seam allowance of the jacket armhole and the seam allowance of the lining armhole. Only seam allowances — not through the visible fabric.
  4. Pull both seam allowances out through the turning hole.
  5. Do a quick running stitch tack: back and forth about 4 times through both seam allowances.
  6. Knot off. Push everything back inside.
  7. Repeat for the other sleeve.

Tack 2: Bottom Hem (Side Seams & Center Back)

This prevents the bottom hem lining from pulling away from the jacket. Three tack points along the bottom:

  1. Reach through the turning hole.
  2. At the left side seam along the bottom hem: grab the jacket seam allowance and the lining seam allowance. Pull out through the hole. Tack with a few running stitches. Knot off.
  3. Repeat at center back.
  4. Repeat at the right side seam.

Part 8: Quick Reference

This Week's Checklist

  • Hand-tack the two neck seam allowances together (shoulder to shoulder)
  • Thread-trace buttonhole marking(s) onto the right side of the jacket
  • Pin lining to facing edges (corners, shoulders, center back, fill in)
  • Sew lining to facings at ½″ (front facing → back neck → other front facing)
  • Pin lining to jacket bottom hem (push hem extension lip down to align raw edges)
  • Sew bottom hem at ½″
  • Turn jacket right side out through the lining turning hole
  • Tuck in front corner bubbles and press hem overhang
  • Position sleeve linings inside jacket sleeves (check seam alignment)
  • Use reach-through technique to join sleeve lining to jacket sleeve at hem
  • Sew sleeve hems at ½″ (both sleeves)
  • Pull sleeve linings back through and press cuff edges
  • Hand-tack underarm/armhole on both sleeves
  • Hand-tack bottom hem at both side seams and center back
  • Get buttonholes done (home machine or professional service)

Final Class (Next Week)

Closing the turning hole by hand and sewing on the button(s). That's it — the jacket is done.
