Part 1: Understanding & Setting the Break

What the break is, why you set it first, how to position it, and how to notch it into your fabric pieces.

What Is the Break?

The break is the point on the jacket front where the lapel begins to fold back. It corresponds to the placement of the highest (or only) button. Everything above the break becomes the visible lapel; everything below stays as the jacket front.

This pattern has a single button. The break defaults to wherever the button/buttonhole was placed on the original pattern — but you can move it.

Set the Break First

Today's pressing technique uses the break as a pivot point — you press in opposite directions above and below it. If you don't set the break position before attaching facings and collars, your pressing will be off and the lapel won't fold cleanly. Set the break first.

How to Position the Break

Option A: If You Added Darts

Your horizontal waist marking from the dart already tells you where your waist is. If you want your break at the waist (the most common placement), simply move the button/buttonhole marking on your paper pattern so it's horizontally aligned with that waist line.

Option B: If You Didn't Add Darts

  1. Measure from high point shoulder (where your neck meets your shoulder, on top) straight down the front of your body.
  2. Decide where you want the jacket to close (your break). Use a front-facing camera to visualize.
  3. Note the measurement.
  4. Add ½″ for the seam allowance at the top. Example: if you measured 16″ on your body, mark 16½″ on the pattern from the top edge.
  5. Move the button/buttonhole marking to that new position on your paper pattern.

Which Option Applies to You?

If you added fish-eye darts in Class 4, use Option A — your waist line is already marked. If you skipped darts, use Option B and measure from your high point shoulder.

Notching the Break on Your Fabric

The break position must be clipped (notched) into the raw front edge of your fabric on:

  • Both front jacket pieces (the raw edge where the facing attaches)
  • Both front facing pieces (the matching raw edge)

How to Transfer the Break Notch

  1. Lay your paper pattern on the jacket front piece. At the break line, make a small clip on the raw front edge.
  2. For the second front: lay both fronts on top of each other, aligned. Clip through the second piece at the same spot.
  3. For the facings: line up the short edge of the jacket front with the facing edge. Clip the facing at the same position.
  4. Repeat for the other facing.

Short Edge — Not the Long Edge

The jacket front has a fold-back extension; the facing does not. Use the short edge of the jacket front to align with the facing when transferring the break notch.
