Parts 2 & 3: Pieces, Families & Assembly

The two-family system, interfacing the facings, and attaching each collar to its correct family.

The Two-Family System

This is the same two-family system from the muslin practice in Class 2, now with your actual fabrics.

Facing Family

  • Front facing (×2)
  • Back neck facing (sew center back seam, press open)
  • Top collar (cut on fold, one piece, interfaced) — goes with this family

Jacket Family

  • Assembled jacket body (fronts and backs already sewn from Class 5)
  • Under collar (two pieces, cut on bias, interfaced — sew center back seam, press open) — goes with this family

Interfacing the Facings (Optional)

Interfacing the front facing and back neck facing adds extra heft and structure at the front of the jacket. If you skip it, the jacket will still be fine — there's already plenty of interfacing elsewhere. However, if you did interface your facings, you'll need to grade the seam allowances on both sides of every seam where two interfaced layers meet (see the Grading section).

Step A: Assemble the Facing Family

  1. Sew the center back seam of the back neck facing. Press open.
  2. Sew front facings to the back neck facing at the shoulders, right sides together, ½″ seam allowance. Press open.

Step B: Under Collar → Jacket

  1. Sew the two under collar pieces together at the center back. Press open.
  2. Line up the center back seam of the under collar with the center back seam of the jacket neck. Pin.
  3. Match each end of the collar to the front notch on the jacket. Pin.
  4. Pin the shoulders and fill in between (middle, middle, middle).
  5. Make sure shoulder seam allowances are pressed open before sewing over them.
  6. Sew at ½″. Use the pivot technique at the shoulders: needle down, foot up, push fullness behind the needle, foot down, continue.
  7. Press the seam open.

Step C: Top Collar → Facing

  1. Mark the center back of the top collar (fold in half, notch or pin).
  2. Line up the center back of the collar with the center back of the facing. Pin.
  3. Match collar ends to the front notches. Pin.
  4. Pin shoulders, fill in between.
  5. Sew at ½″ with the same shoulder pivot technique.
  6. Press the seam open.

Lighter Fabrics Without Facing Interfacing

If you didn't interface your facings and you're using a lighter-weight fabric, the facing will feel more fluid and slippery than the interfaced collar. Take extra care when sewing to make sure the lighter fabric doesn't bunch up underneath the stiffer collar piece.

Developing Feel

A big part of leveling up as a sewist is learning to anticipate what's going under the presser foot even when you can't see it. Always lift the collar and reach underneath to feel for bumps, closed seam allowances, or fabric folds before they hit the needle.
