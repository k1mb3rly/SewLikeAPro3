Part 4: Welt Pockets & Quick Reference

Pocket placement options, edit decision checklist, and reminders before cutting.

Welt Pocket Options

The original jacket pattern came with patch pockets, which have been removed for this class. You will use the welt technique practiced in class. Placement and quantity are entirely up to you.

Placement Options

  • Standard: two welt pockets on the lower front, one on each side, horizontal
  • Angled: same position but tilted at a slight diagonal
  • Vertical: turned 90° for a different look
  • Single exterior pocket (one side only)
  • Interior pocket (inside the jacket, accessible when open)
  • Any combination of the above

Tip

The welt construction is identical regardless of placement or angle — the same pattern pieces and techniques from the practice welt apply.

Edit Decision Checklist

Change sleeve lengthOptionalUpper + lower sleeve, main + lining
Add fish-eye dartsOptionalFront + back, main + lining (all 4)

Reminders Before Cutting

  • If cutting at home, take a photo and email the instructor with any questions before you cut.
  • Lining and main patterns are SEPARATE — look for the labels. You need both.
  • "Cut 2" on a pattern means cut through two layers to get two pieces — not trace the pattern twice.
  • Follow grain line and fold directions marked on each pattern piece.
  • Don't forget the 2″ sleeve hem interfacing strips — they're easy to overlook.
