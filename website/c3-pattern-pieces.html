Part 1: Master Pattern Piece List

Every piece you need to cut for your blazer — shell, lining, and interfacing.

Pattern Size Groups

The pattern is divided into two size groups. Identify which group your size falls into, then count out to your size from the smallest in that group.

Interfacing Pieces

Cut from weft fusible interfacing. Cut each piece individually, then fuse to its corresponding fabric piece (do not block-fuse).

Pattern PieceNotes
Back InterfacingIncluded in pattern. Mimics the upper back of the jacket.
FrontUse the same main front pattern. Full front gets interfaced.
Top CollarUse the same top collar pattern.
Under CollarUse the same under collar pattern.
Sleeve Hem StripsTrace just the bottom 2″ of both the upper and lower sleeve patterns onto separate paper. Cut these strips in interfacing.
Welt InterfacingSame as the practice welt. One set per welt pocket.

Interfacing Method

Cut each interfacing piece individually to its pattern shape, then fuse to the corresponding fabric piece. Do NOT block-fuse (fusing a large slab of interfacing to a large slab of fabric before cutting). It wastes interfacing, especially for pieces like the back where only the upper portion is interfaced.

Tip

If fusing terrifies you, some shops will cut and fuse your pieces for around $30 — but learning to do it yourself is straightforward and worth the skill.
